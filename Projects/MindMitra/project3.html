MindMitra

Designing an empathetic mental health support app tailored for Indian college students. Bridging the gap between need and access through research-driven, culturally relevant solutions.

"Mental health is not a destination, but a process. It's about how you drive, not where you're going."
— Noam Shpancer

Research & Insights

Objective: Understand mental health challenges, barriers, and needs of Indian college students.

Desk Research Highlights

  • 44.7% of students experience depression; 65.44% report significant stress.
  • Only 10% seek professional help due to stigma and lack of awareness.
  • India has 0.75 psychiatrists per 100,000 people vs. WHO’s recommended 3 per 100,000.

Key User Insights

  • Preference for anonymous, digital solutions.
  • Cultural relevance and emotional tone are critical for engagement.
  • Students rely on informal support; professional help seen as last resort.
"One in five college students reports experiencing mental health issues each year, yet fewer than 20% receive treatment."
— National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI)

Ideation & Features

How Might We... Help students manage stress without feeling judged?

Anonymous Onboarding

No personal info required; use emojis for quick personalization.

Mood & Stress Tracker

Quick mood check-in with emoji scale; AI-driven insights based on trends.

Emotional Wellness Content

Short culturally relevant videos/articles, available in regional languages.

Peer & Expert Support

Anonymous forums and optional chat with counselors.

Immediate Crisis Assistance

One-tap access to helplines and emergency contacts.

Prototype & Usability Testing

Method: Conducted with 5 students to test navigation, emotional tone, and usability.

  • Observed confusion with mood tags; simplified UI accordingly.
  • Users felt more comfortable with a prominent "Skip" option in onboarding.
  • Positive feedback on color palette and gentle microinteractions.

Iterations: Improved button sizes, added tooltips, reduced text density.

Key Learnings & Future Improvements

  • Designing for emotional safety requires careful microinteractions and tone.
  • Continuous user feedback is crucial; plan regular usability tests post-launch.
  • Future: Integrate AI-driven chat support, expand language options.

Takeaway: "Designing with empathy is designing for impact."
